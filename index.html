Introduction

In this lesson, we will learn how to solve linear equations. This is a fundamental skill in algebra that will help you in many areas of math and science.

Theory

A linear equation is an equation between two variables that gives a straight line when plotted on a graph. The standard form of a linear equation is ax + b = c.

Here, x is the variable, a is the coefficient, and b is the constant.

Steps to Solve a Linear Equation

  • Isolate the variable on one side of the equation.
  • Perform inverse operations to solve for the variable.

Examples

Example 1

Solve for x: 2x + 3 = 7

Step 1: Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x + 3 - 3 = 7 - 3

Step 2: Simplify: 2x = 4

Step 3: Divide both sides by 2: x = 2
